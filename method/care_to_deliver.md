# Care to Deliver

Care to Deliver is a framework for restoring movement and trust in teams.

It focuses on creating the human conditions necessary for effective collaboration and delivery.

## The Principle

Most organizations try to deliver first and care later.

Care to Deliver reverses that:

Care → Trust → Teamwork → Delivery

When teams care about each other and share openly, trust grows and collaboration becomes natural.

## The Method

### 1. Observe Human Signals

Before analyzing processes, observe people.

Look for:

- engagement
- silence
- non-verbal reactions
- openness or defensiveness

Human behavior reveals the health of the system.

---

### 2. Evaluate Agreements

Understand what the team believes it agreed to.

Look for clarity in:

- expectations
- communication practices
- responsibilities
- goals

Many teams fail because agreements exist only in someone’s head.

---

### 3. Evaluate Evaluations

Examine how success is measured.

Check whether metrics reflect:

- real value
- progress toward goals
- learning opportunities

Bad metrics often create fear instead of improvement.

---

### 4. Evaluate Collaboration

Observe how people actually work together.

Look at:

- standups
- planning meetings
- retrospectives
- daily conversations

Healthy teams challenge ideas and support each other.

---

### 5. Introduce Movement

When systems stagnate, introduce change.

Examples:

- restructure team interactions
- shift focus to outcomes
- adjust rituals
- experiment with new approaches

Movement exposes hidden problems.

---

### 6. Encourage Sharing

Sharing is the foundation of trust.

Encourage people to share:

- ideas
- questions
- doubts
- context
- mistakes

The first person to share often needs courage.

Once one person shares, others follow.

---

### 7. Build Trust → Unlock Teamwork

When people feel safe to share:

- vulnerability appears
- trust grows
- accountability becomes collective
- teams begin to move forward together

At that point, delivery improves naturally.
