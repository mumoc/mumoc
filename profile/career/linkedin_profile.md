## ABOUT SECTION

I help teams move forward by building the conditions where people can do their best work together.

For 15+ years, I've worked across every layer of software organizations—engineering, design, QA, product, operations, eCommerce, and people leadership. I left a COO role because the organization asked me to stop prioritizing people. I chose integrity over advancement and returned to engineering to deepen my technical expertise while continuing to improve my methods.

**What I do now:**

I'm a full-stack engineer at Dealerware working across backend (Ruby/Rails), frontend (React), and mobile (Flutter). But my real focus is on systems-level work: designing data infrastructure, bridging teams, improving communication, and building reliable foundations that enable others to move faster.

**My framework: Care → Trust → Teamwork → Delivery**

Most organizations try to deliver first and care later. I reverse that. When teams care about each other and share openly, trust grows and collaboration becomes natural. This isn't soft—it directly impacts technical decisions, velocity, and business outcomes.

**On AI in engineering:**

I'm actively building AI-assisted workflows using Claude as a cognitive collaborator. My approach is disciplined: define business logic first, structure work with clear acceptance criteria, then use AI at each phase. I version rules and practices alongside code to ensure quality scales. AI amplifies what already works—it doesn't replace judgment, accountability, or team trust.

**What you'll find here:**

- Thinking on team dynamics, leadership, and organizational systems
- Technical posts on architecture, AI integration, and systems design
- Lessons learned building products, platforms, and people

I write from lived experience, not theory. I'm interested in thoughtful conversation about how we build better teams and organizations.

Based in Colima, Mexico. Available for consulting, technical advising, or conversation about team dynamics and technical leadership.

> The difference between AI that scales impact and AI that scales chaos is this:
> 
> **Chaos:** One person uses AI to move faster. The team waits. Context breaks. Quality suffers.
> 
> **Scale:** Teams integrate AI as a peer. Business logic is clear. Acceptance criteria are explicit. AI helps the whole team move together.
> 
> The technology is the same. The approach is completely different.

### Post C: Technical Debt as Culture
> Technical debt is not about code.
> 
> It's about how your team feels about maintenance. It's whether people care. It's whether you treat improvements as strategic or cosmetic.
> 
> I've seen teams eliminate technical debt not by refactoring everything at once, but by weaving improvements into every feature. Over time, the system becomes better. The team becomes more engaged. Quality improves.
> 
> Debt is a culture problem first.
