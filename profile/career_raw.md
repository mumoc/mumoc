## Core Career Insight #1 — Creating Your Own Path

> "No one will come and tell you what to do or what you should do.
> It will be more YOU looking for what you need, learning to keep in sight what you really want.
> Then, if you can do that, everything is possible."
> — Mumo

This happened 4–5 times across his career. Not once. Repeatedly.

The pattern:
1. See a need the organization has that nobody is filling
2. Recognize you're already doing the work without the title or compensation
3. Articulate clearly what you want, why it makes sense, what the boundaries are
4. Propose it. Back it with evidence. Get the support.

Mumo doesn't climb ladders. He builds rungs that didn't exist — then hands them to the organization and moves on. This is a founder behavior inside someone else's company.

### System 1: Custom Top Grading (Hiring & Promotion Methodology)

**File:** `content/drafts/TopGrading.xlsx`

**Origin:** First principles from Crowd Interactive / MagmaLabs years.
**Refined:** COO role, applied at full organizational scale.
**Formalized:** Learning Manager period, moving toward CLO.

#### What It Is

A structured hiring and promotion methodology based on Top Grading (the industry framework),
but dramatically simplified and adapted for real organizational use.

The original: 12 steps, weeks of process, heavy interviewer training required.
Mumo's version: 6 steps, faster, standardized, actually used.

#### The 5 Components (Tabs)

**1. Criteria Definitions**
18 evaluation criteria across 4 dimensions:
- **Application** — Resume strength, preparation, experience relevance
- **Work Style** — Reliability, goes above duty, thoughtful communicator, bias toward results, grit, stress handling, intellectual curiosity, scalable process builder
- **Personal Drive** — Self motivation, personal growth, coachability
- **Cultural Fit** — Airport test, energy, alignment, humility
- **Role Specific** — Technical skills for the specific position

Each criterion has a precise behavioral definition — not just a label.

**2. Scoring Rubric (5 = average)**
The key innovation. A 0-10 scale where:
- 0 = Worst you've ever seen (actively oriented in opposite direction)
- 2 = Very weak (devoid of characteristic, unwilling to acknowledge importance)
- 4 = Below average (unaware of criteria's importance)
- **5 = Average of the CURRENT TEAM** — not the world, not your peers. This team, right now.
- 6 = Above average
- 7 = Strong (better than average, room for growth)
- 8 = Very strong (constant thirst to improve)
- 9 = Exceptional (defining characteristic + ability to coach others to this level)
- 10 = Best you've seen (redefines understanding of the category)

**The reframe:** 5 doesn't mean "mediocre." It means "fits our current standard."
Above 5 = will raise the team. Below 5 = will lower it. Hire to always improve the average.

**3. Grading Sheet**
Multi-interviewer scoring sheet with:
- One row per criterion
- One column per interviewer
- Automatic average calculation
- Observations column for qualitative notes
- Per-interviewer and overall averages

Shows real use: includes an actual candidate evaluation (anonymized as "Michael Specter")
with Mumo and three other interviewers scoring across dimensions.

**4. Journey (Process Checklist)**
Step-by-step hiring checklist:
Job Requisition → CV Review → First Contact → Interview 3x1 → Technical Test (optional) → Decision
Each step has specific sub-tasks tracked as done/not done.

**5. Question Suggestions**
Curated behavioral interview questions designed to surface:
- Initiative beyond formal scope
- Empathy-driven decision making
- Learning orientation
- Failure and growth
- Reference check questions (behavioral, specific)

### System 2: Performance & Potential Definitions Matrix

**File:** `content/drafts/Performance and Potential Definitions Matrix.xlsx`

**Purpose:** A tool to translate subjective performance intuitions into objective,
calibrated assessments for career growth and talent review.

**The core problem it solves:**
Most performance evaluations are either purely subjective ("I feel like they're doing well")
or purely metric-based (hit/miss on KPIs). Neither captures the full picture.
This matrix creates a shared language for evaluation that anyone can use consistently.

#### The 4 Components (Tabs)

**1. Matrix (9-box grid)**
3x3 grid with:
- X axis: Potential (Low / Medium / High)
- Y axis: Performance (Under / Effective / Outstanding)

9 zones numbered 1-9, each with specific behavioral definitions across 4 traits.

Result classifications:
- Zone 1-3 (Outstanding): Ready for more / Ready now / High potential
- Zone 4-6 (Effective): Standard performer / Needs coaching / Ready for new role
- Zone 7-9 (Under): Recovery plan / Needs development / May not be a fit

Special indicators:
- "↓ Too soon: 3 Months" — don't promote yet, let them develop
- "★ Must leave company" — clear, documented, not subjective

**2. Traits (4 evaluation dimensions)**
Every person evaluated across 4 traits, each with 5 levels:

**Mastery** (skill level):
Beginner → Advanced Beginner → Competent → Proficient → Expert

**Impact** (reach and influence):
Self → Project → Pod/Projects → Area → Company & Beyond

**Growth & Recognition** (learning orientation + community contribution):
Learning → Apply Knowledge → Enhance Knowledge → Share Knowledge → Promotes Development

**Performance** (goal achievement):
Below → Beneath → Standard → Exceeds → Elite

Each level has precise behavioral descriptions — not just labels.
This is what converts subjective impressions into objective placements.

**3. Glossary**
Shared definitions for all key terms:
- High Potentials (HiPo)
- Emerging Talent
- Readiness
- Talent Gap
- Nomination
- Recovery Plan
- Leaving Soon
- Coaching vs. Mentoring (with important distinction: coaching requires certification)

**4. Talent Review Generalities**
Who to rate, who not to rate, and leader process steps:
- Identify succession candidates (6mo / 1yr / 2yr / 5yr horizons)
- Rate based on defined criteria
- Nominate and defend candidates
- Categorize into HiPo / ET / Recovery / Leaving Soon
- Identify talent gaps and priorities

This is not HR work. This is systems engineering applied to people.

The same thinking Mumo applies to technical architecture —
define clear interfaces, calibrate metrics, create feedback loops, document for reuse —
he applied to talent systems.

Most organizations have vague, inconsistent, political performance conversations.
Mumo built infrastructure for those conversations:
- Shared language
- Calibrated scales
- Behavioral definitions
- Process checklists
- Multi-evaluator aggregation

And critically: he built these to be used by anyone, not just him.
The whole point was organizational self-sufficiency — systems that work without him in the room.

That's the same philosophy as founding MagmaHackers and designing career paths.
Build it. Document it. Hand it to the organization. Move on.
