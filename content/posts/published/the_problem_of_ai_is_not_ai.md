# The problem of AI is not AI

_"What do we do NOW!!!?"_

Nothing else but uncomfortable silence.

_"Just tell me what to do and I'll do it."_

Everyone froze.

No movement.

No decisions.

No coordination.

Clearly everyone was trying to appear busy, evading each other look.

_"If AI can write my code better than me…_
_then what are we going to do?"_

Sepulcral silence again.

---

## The delusion

_"I deliver a lot of tickets!!! I'm great"_

That's **the fear**.

And it's deeply rooted in us. Not only rational, but a deeper fear.

Just think about it, try to understand these people context:

_if the only thing I do is write code,_
_and AI does it faster and better ..._
_of course I should be worried._

That's a reality, but what it reflects is deeper.

The problem isn't AI. The problem is **your value**.

If your value comes from merely execution and NOT **thinking**

Of course you're at risk.

> You must know at least one... one peer with that trait
>
> That one that "deliver" tickets by regurgitating code
>
> of course you know someone

---

## The valuable asset

I think different, veeeeeery different:

The value comes from **the context**

Being valuable, in any situation, means:
- Understanding the problem
- Connecting the pieces
- Giving direction

AI can generate parts.

But the amalgam...

That's **human**.

> Do you know one of those people that move mountains?
>
> One of those you proudly say "That's my leader"?
>
> That's only human, and it comes thanks to empathy

---

## A bigger and costlier problem

Someone told me:

"But we have Codex, we can just ask it about the project..."

Yes, you can.

But AI only sees what is implemented.
It doesn't understand the business.
It doesn't know the goal.

It can explain.
Analyze.
Even improve.

But only if you know what to ask.

Only if you bring context.

Because if the person asking doesn't understand…
and what's written isn't clear…

**how do you expect AI to know where to go?**

You might be familiar with this scenario:

Someone asked a simple question.
About a flow.

The answer came fast.
Confident.

And **completely wrong**.

A long response about analysis, coordination, migrations, integrations… irrelevant things.

It was obvious:

copy-paste from AI.

AI did its job.

The human didn't.

No understanding.
No filtering.
No context.

> I know you know someone, I bet..

---

## The old unreliable

Like an old classic movie, the defacto problems:
- Documentation missing.
- Code no one understands.
- Comments explaining *how*, not *why*.
- Tickets without context.
- Commits without story.
- Knowledge scattered everywhere.

These problems were always there.

Across teams.

Across roles.

Across levels.

---

## The REAL PROBLEM

The question is another again:

Is this only a developer problem? a qa problem? a designer problem? even a manager problem?

Or also a **"leadership" problem**?

Because if your job is to think,
to give direction,
to provide context...

and you reduce it to copy-paste...

then yes,

AI will replace you.

It won't care about your title, your position, or how many things you're delivering.
It won't care about your name or how well you hide the gap.

It will reach you and it will replace you


AI is not the problem. It just makes it visible.

The problem… is us.
