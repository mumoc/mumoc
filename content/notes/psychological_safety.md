# Psychological Safety

Psychological safety is the feeling that it is safe to speak openly within a team.

It is not a policy.

It is a **felt condition**.

## When Psychological Safety Exists

People feel comfortable:

- asking questions
- admitting mistakes
- challenging ideas
- sharing incomplete thoughts
- experimenting

These behaviors create continuous learning.

## When It Does Not Exist

People begin to:

- hide mistakes
- avoid difficult conversations
- move discussions to private channels
- stop asking questions

Teams appear functional, but progress slows down.

## Important Balance

Psychological safety must exist alongside accountability.

Safety without accountability leads to complacency.

Accountability without safety leads to fear.

Strong teams maintain both.
